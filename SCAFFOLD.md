## **Key Observations from This Scaffold**

1. **Authority Mediation**

   * Every transition is mediated: divine, institutional, or cultural authority frames the meaning of experience.
   * Ordinary readers rarely interpret raw experience alone; texts scaffold comprehension.

2. **Cognitive Scaffolding**

   * Argument structures, rhetorical devices, and recurring idioms provide **mental frameworks** for reasoning across domains.
   * Pamphlets act like compressed cognitive “schemas” for readers: moral, legal, theological.

3. **Multi-dimensional Integration**

   * Politics, law, ethics, and theology were rarely treated as separate. Authority in one domain informed authority in another.
   * Understanding authority in 1640 England, for example, required literacy in biblical metaphor, legal reasoning, and civic ethics.

4. **Semantic Drift and Lexical Anchoring**

   * Widespread, inexpensive texts capture **how words change in meaning** across decades.
   * They also reveal how **moral, legal, and spiritual concepts were standardized or contested**.

5. **Evidential Richness**

   * Pamphlets and ephemeral texts are particularly valuable because they capture **public discourse in real time**, including disputes, persuasion, outrage, and compliance.
